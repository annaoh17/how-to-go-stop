Gameplay Explanation!

Before playing, decide if you want to play with chips, money, or nothing at all. If you choose to play with chips, the number can vary depending on your preferences, the rules you've set, and the number of rounds you have decided to play. The goal is to accumulate the most points and chips to win the game! If you prefer to play with money, that is entirely up to you and how you want to gamble. However, please be mindful when gambling. This option is not one I recommend! If you decide to play for points only, then keep on reading!

The first person to play is the dealer. During each player's turn:

—Example of Collected Card Organization—

  • Play a card from your hand that matches a card on the table.
  • After placing your card down, flip the top card from the deck and place it in the center with the rest of the cards on the table.
  • Even if you don't have a match in your hand, you still must place a card down and flip one from the deck.
  • If you're lucky, the card you flip might match something on the table — and you can collect those cards.
  • After you're done your turn, collect any cards that you matched.
  • Organize your collected cards in a way so both you and your opponent can see them.
  • The game continues until a player stops the round — either because they have enough points — or there are no more cards left to play.
  • Whoever wins, collect your chips, money, and/or bragging rights.
  • The winner becomes the new dealer for the next game.
Other conditions:
  • If there are already two cards of a set on the table, and you have one of the cards in that matching set, you decide which of the two cards you want to collect.
  • If there are already two cards of a set on the table, and you flip off a card from the deck that is a part of that said set, you have to leave that card on the table and stack those three cards together.
